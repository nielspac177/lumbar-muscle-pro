# Literature & Novelty Assessment

**Search date:** 2026-05-26 · **Source:** PubMed (E-utilities, relevance-sorted).
**Question:** How novel is "iliopsoas/paraspinal muscle volume & quality → PROs after
lumbar decompression"?

**Key signal:** PROMIS-PF + muscle + spine returns only **13** papers, and
sarcopenia + lumbar *decompression* only **16** — the precise intersection we occupy
is thinly populated, even though the broad theme is active.

## Closest competitors (the gap)

- **Song et al., Spine J 2022** — *Muscle health & PROs after lumbar microdiscectomy
  (early results)*, HSS. MRI-based lumbar **muscle grade**, microdiscectomy only,
  early follow-up. → We differ: **volumetric 3D** (not a grade), **multiple muscle
  groups incl. iliopsoas + gluteus**, **decompression broadly**, **1-year** horizon.
- **"Paraspinal sarcopenia predicts PROs following open but not MIS" (Spine J 2026)**
  — moderation by *procedure type*. → Different angle; we use a **leg-pain
  negative control** for mechanistic specificity, not procedure stratification.
- **"Influence of Paraspinal Sarcopenia on PROs Following Lumbar Fusion" (Clin Spine
  Surg 2026)** — **fusion**, not decompression.
- **Zhang/various, Sci Rep 2019; NASSJ 2026** — sarcopenia + **MIS decompression**;
  generally multifidus / L3 psoas **single-slice CSA**.
- **"Hip, Abdomen, and Paraspinal Muscle Morphologies… Pain & Disability" (Spine 2025)**
  — correlational, includes hip muscles; not a decompression-outcome / PROMIS-PF
  recovery design.

## Established vs. our contribution

**Established (NOT novel):** that paraspinal sarcopenia/atrophy is broadly associated
with worse spine-surgery PROs.

**Our defensible novelty (incremental, combination-based):**
1. **Iliopsoas as a functional (hip-flexor/gait) predictor** — most prior work treats
   psoas CSA merely as a whole-body sarcopenia marker, or focuses on multifidus.
2. **Volumetric, multi-muscle 3D segmentation** (iliopsoas + deep back + gluteus
   medius) rather than single-axial-slice CSA at L3/L4.
3. **Decompression-only cohort with PROMIS Physical Function + MCID responder analysis.**
4. **Leg-pain negative-control dissociation** — testing that muscle predicts
   *functional* but not *radicular* recovery; not seen in the retrieved literature and
   it doubles as a confounding check.
5. **Spine-normalized sarcopenia index** (muscle ÷ vertebral-body volume) enabled by
   simultaneous vertebra/disc segmentation.

## Verdict
**Sufficiently novel for a CNS abstract** (a venue that accepts focused
single-institution retrospective work), provided we foreground items 2–4. The field is
moving fast (multiple 2026 papers), so the framing must be sharp and the negative-control
dissociation should be the headline. Citations below should be verified against full text
before manuscript submission.
